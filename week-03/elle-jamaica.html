Jamaica

Introduction

The island of Jamaica was discovered by Christopher Columbus in 1494. The Spanish settled there in the early 16th century.

Population

In 2023, the population was 2,820,982.

Religion

In 2011, the most popular religion in Jamaica was Protestant, with a total of 64.8% of the population practicing the religion.

Ethnicity (2011)

  • Black (92.1%)
  • Mixed (6.1%)
  • East Indian (0.8%)
  • other (0.4%)
  • unspecified (0.7%)

Environment

Jamaica has a tropical climate and the island suffers from some environmental issues.

Current Issues

The island suffers from heavy rates of deforestation. Other current issues are pollution and damage to the coral reefs.

Climate

The climate is hot and humid, which is typical for a tropical island.

Waste and Recycling (2016)

The municipal solid waste generated anually is 1,051,695 tons.

Transportation

In 2020, there were no registered air carriers on the island.

Airports (2021)

Jamaica contains 28 airports. 11 of the airports have paved runways, while the other 17 have unpaved runways.

Roadways

In 2017, the island contained 25,595 km of roadways.

Major Seaports

  • Kingston
  • Montego Bay
  • Port Antonio
  • Port Esquivel
  • Port Kaiser
  • Rocky Point
